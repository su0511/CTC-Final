Desire is a chain around the neck. Gold shone in his dreams and clouded his soul. Soft laughter drew him deeper into the maze. The fragrance of wealth dulled the river’s whisper. Greedy hands gathered illusions. He forgot the songs of birds and the stars’ embrace. His smile became hollow, his heart a closed jar. Wandering between silken beds and golden coins. Each pleasure consumed, another thirst awakened. The river was lost behind walls of noise. He sold his peace for games and silk robes. The taste of wine dulled the voice of awakening. He who sought freedom became the most bound. Days passed in golden blindness. The boy who loved trees now bartered lives. His dreams bled dry under velvet nights. The river, once near, now faded into silence. In the depths of pleasure, he drowned unseen. Despair clung to his shadow like a second skin. The river waited patiently, speaking only to silence.
